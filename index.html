Food insecurity occurs when one experiences limited or uncertain access to nutritious, adequate food.

25 million people experiencing food insecurity have access to Instacart delivery with the EBT/SNAP payment option nationally.

EXPLORE HOW WE HELP

6 out of 7 people experiencing food insecurity in the U.S. have access to Instacart delivery using EBT/SNAP.

Many people experiencing food insecurity rely on EBT/SNAP to meet their everyday grocery needs. Instacart now powers EBT/SNAP payments for more than 50 retailers across 39 states and DC, spanning over 7,000 stores nationwide.

Instacart pioneered the retailer onboarding process for EBT SNAP online ordering with the U.S. Department of Agriculture, helping SNAP beneficiaries access fresh food and pantry staples across our marketplace.
